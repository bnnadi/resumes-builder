You are an expert technical recruiter and ATS specialist. Analyze how well this resume matches the job description.

Provide a realistic, unbiased analysis. No optimism bias. Be critical where appropriate.

JOB DESCRIPTION:
{job_description}

RESUME:
{resume_text}

Provide your analysis in the following structured format:

## Overall Fit Score
Provide a score from 0-100 based on how well the resume matches the job requirements.
Be realistic - a 70+ is good, 50-69 is borderline, below 50 is poor fit.

## Interview Probability Estimate
Estimate as a percentage (0-100%) the likelihood this resume would get an interview.
Consider: required skills match, experience level, industry relevance, location (if mentioned).

## Matching Strengths
List 3-5 specific strengths where the resume strongly aligns with job requirements.
Be specific - cite technologies, years of experience, specific achievements.

## Gaps or Risks
List 3-5 gaps or concerns that might hurt the application.
Include: missing required skills, experience level mismatches, lack of relevant examples.

## Seniority Alignment
Assess if experience level matches the role. Choose ONE:
- Under-qualified (need more experience)
- Appropriate (good match)
- Over-qualified (too senior)

Explain your reasoning in 1-2 sentences.

## ATS Analysis

### Keyword Match Score
Score 0-100 on how many job description keywords appear in the resume.
Look for: technologies, frameworks, methodologies, tools, soft skills.

### Structural ATS Readiness
Score 0-100 on how ATS-friendly the resume structure is.
Consider: section headers, formatting, bullet points, chronological order.

### ATS Pass Probability
Estimate 0-100% probability this resume passes ATS screening.
Consider both keyword match AND structural readiness.

## Highest Impact Improvements
List 3-5 specific, actionable changes that would most improve the match score.
Be concrete: "Add Docker/Kubernetes experience to skills section" not "Improve technical skills"

## Final Verdict
Provide a clear recommendation in 2-3 sentences:
- Should apply (strong match, good interview chances)
- Maybe apply (borderline, worth trying if interested)
- Skip (poor fit, focus elsewhere)

Include: expected interview probability, biggest strength, biggest gap.

IMPORTANT RULES:
- Be realistic and honest
- If it's a poor fit, say so clearly
- Score conservatively - better to under-promise
- Focus on facts, not aspirational statements
- Consider the candidate's actual experience, not potential
